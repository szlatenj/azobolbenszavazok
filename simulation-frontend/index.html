Modszertan

Ez a szimulator Monte Carlo modszert hasznal a 2026-os magyar orszaggyulesi valasztasok elorejelzesere. A modell $N = 20\,000$ szimulalt valasztast futtat le, es minden alkalommal veletlen valtozokkal modositja a kozvelemenykutatasi adatokat, hogy felmerhesse a bizonytalansagot. A megkozelites hasonlo a Nate Silver-fele FiveThirtyEight modellhez, a magyar valasztasi rendszerre adaptialva.

1. A magyar valasztasi rendszer

Az Orszaggyulesnek 199 kepviseloje van:

  • 106 egyeni valasztokeruleti (OEVK) mandatum — tobbsegi rendszer (FPTP), egy fordulo. A legtobb szavazatot kapo jelolt nyer.
  • 93 orszagos listai mandatum — aranyos elosztas a d'Hondt modszer alapjan, toredekszavazatokkal kiegeszitve.

2. Kozvelemenykutatas-osszesites

Minden kozveleemnykutatas sulyozott atlaga harom tenyezo szorzata:

$$ w_i = w_i^{\text{ido}} \cdot w_i^{\text{minta}} \cdot w_i^{\text{minoseg}} $$

Idoszerusegi suly — exponencialis bomlas felezesi idovel $\tau$ (alapertek: 14 nap):

$$ w_i^{\text{ido}} = \exp\!\Bigl(-\frac{\ln 2}{\tau} \cdot \Delta t_i\Bigr) $$

Mintanagysag-suly:

$$ w_i^{\text{minta}} = \sqrt{\frac{n_i}{1000}} $$

Minosegi suly $w_i^{\text{minoseg}} \in [0, 1]$: kutatohoz rendelt ertek. A hazeffektus-korrekciok szinten alkalmazasra kerulnek a nyers kozvelemenykutatasi adatokon.

Az osszesitett szavazataranyt normalizaljuk, hogy osszeguk 1 legyen. A sulyozott szorast $\sigma_p^{\text{poll}}$ szamoljuk minden partra, de minimum $\sigma_{\text{floor}}$ (alapertek: 2%).

3. Korrelalt orszagos szavazatarany-huzas

Az atlagos szavazataranyokat $\bar{\pi}_k$ logit-terbe transzformaljuk:

$$ \mu_k = \text{logit}(\bar{\pi}_k) = \ln\!\frac{\bar{\pi}_k}{1 - \bar{\pi}_k} $$

A teljes szoras logit-terben a kutatasbeli szoras es a szisztematikus hiba osszege:

$$ \sigma_k = \sigma_k^{\text{poll}} + \sigma_{\text{national}} $$

A kovarianciamartix $\Sigma$ a kovetkezo korrelaciokon alapul:

  • Fidesz ↔ fo ellenzek (Tisza): $\rho = -0{,}70$ (eros negativ korrelacio)
  • Kispartok egymas kozott: $\rho = +0{,}30$
  • Fidesz ↔ kispartok: $\rho = -0{,}20$
$$ \Sigma = \text{diag}(\boldsymbol{\sigma}) \cdot R \cdot \text{diag}(\boldsymbol{\sigma}) $$

Egy szimulacioban $N$ darab mintavetel tortenik logit-terben, majd szisztematikus kozveleemnykutatasi hiba hozzaadasa (ami a Fidesz-t es az ellenzeket ellentetesen tolja el):

$$ \mathbf{z}^{(i)} \sim \mathcal{N}(\boldsymbol{\mu}, \Sigma), \quad \varepsilon^{(i)} \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma_{\text{national}}) $$ $$ z_{\text{Fidesz}}^{(i)} \mathrel{+}= \varepsilon^{(i)}, \quad z_{\text{Tisza}}^{(i)} \mathrel{-}= \varepsilon^{(i)} $$

A visszatranszformalas szigmoid fugvennyel es normalizalassal tortenik:

$$ \pi_k^{(i)} = \frac{\text{sigmoid}(z_k^{(i)})}{\sum_{j} \text{sigmoid}(z_j^{(i)})} $$

4. Swing modell: orszagosbol keruletszintre

A keruleti szavazataranyok a 2022-es bazisvonalra epulnek, modositva az orszagos swing-gel, regionalis es keruleti zajjal:

$$ s_{d,k}^{(i)} = b_{d,k} + \underbrace{(\pi_k^{(i)} - \bar{b}_k)}_{\text{orszagos swing}} + \underbrace{\eta_{r(d),k}^{(i)}}_{\text{regionalis}} + \underbrace{\epsilon_{d,k}^{(i)}}_{\text{keruleti}} $$

ahol $b_{d,k}$ a 2022-es keruletszintu szavazataranyt jeloli, $\bar{b}_k$ az orszagos bazis, es:

$$ \eta_{r,k} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\, \sigma_{\text{regional}}), \quad \epsilon_{d,k} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\, \sigma_{\text{district}}) $$

A 106 kerulet 8 regionba van sorolva (Budapest, Pest, ENy-Dunantul, Kozep-Dunantul, Del-Dunantul, Eszak-Mo., Eszak-Alfold, Del-Alfold). Az egy regionhoz tartozo keruletek osztoznak a regionalis zajon.

5. Varos/videk differencialt swing

Minden kerulethez egy $u_d \in [0, 1]$ urbanizacios pontszam van rendelve (Budapest: 0.90–1.0, megyeszekhelyek: 0.6–0.8, videk: 0.1–0.3). A centralt urbanizacio ($\tilde{u}_d = u_d - \bar{u}$) biztositja, hogy a hatas orszagos szinten zero-sum legyen:

$$ s_{d,k}^{(i)} \mathrel{+}= \gamma_k \cdot \tilde{u}_d $$

ahol $\gamma_k$ a part-specifikus varosi swing parameter (pl. $\gamma_{\text{Fidesz}} = -0{,}05$ azt jelenti, hogy a Fidesz 5 szazalekpontot veszit a legurbanizaltabb keruletekben a legvidekiebbhez kepest).

Budapest extra swing: egy kulon parameter $\beta_{\text{BP}}$ amely kizarolag a 18 budapesti keruletben hat:

$$ s_{d,\text{Tisza}}^{(i)} \mathrel{+}= \beta_{\text{BP}} \cdot \mathbb{1}_{d \in \text{BP}}, \quad s_{d,\text{Fidesz}}^{(i)} \mathrel{-}= \beta_{\text{BP}} \cdot \mathbb{1}_{d \in \text{BP}} $$

Vegul a szavazataranyokat $[0{,}005;\, 0{,}95]$ intervallumra vagjuk es ujranormalizaljuk, hogy osszeguk 1 legyen.

6. Reszveteli differencialas

A keruleti osszes szavazatszamot modosithatja a varos/videk reszveteli eltolodas:

$$ V_d' = V_d \cdot \bigl(1 + \delta_{\text{urban}} \cdot u_d + \delta_{\text{rural}} \cdot (1 - u_d)\bigr) $$

ahol $\delta_{\text{urban}}$ es $\delta_{\text{rural}}$ a reszveteli valtozas parameterei.

7. Szavazatmegosztas (lista vs. OEVK elteres)

A valasztok ket szavazatot adnak le: egyet az egyeni jeloltre (OEVK), egyet az orszagos listra. Ezek elterhetnek — peldaul egy Mi Hazank-szavazo a keruletben mas jeloltre (pl. „egyeb”) szavazhat, de a listan a Mi Hazankra. A modell ezt ugy kezeli, hogy minden parthoz megadhato egy celpart es egy szazalek: az OEVK-szavazok $f_k$ hanyada a listan a celpartra szavaz.

A listai szavazataranyok az OEVK-bazisbol szarmaznak, modositva az atszavazasokkal:

$$ \text{lista}_k^{(i)} = \pi_k^{(i)} - f_k \cdot \pi_k^{(i)} + \sum_{j:\,\text{cel}(j)=k} f_j \cdot \pi_j^{(i)} $$

ahol $f_k \in [0, 0{,}30]$ a $k$ part OEVK-szavazoibol a celpartra atszavazok aranya. Peldaul ha $f_{\text{Mi Hazank}} = 0{,}10$ es cel $=$ egyeb, akkor a Mi Hazank OEVK-szavazoibol 10% a listan az „egyeb” partra szavaz. Alapertek: 0 (nincs atszavazas).

8. OEVK szimulacio es toredekszavazatok

Minden keruletben a legtobb szavazatot kapo jelolt nyer. A toredekszavazatok:

  • Vesztes jeloltek: osszes szavazatuk toredekszavazat.
  • Gyoztes jelolt:
$$ F_{\text{winner}} = \max\bigl(v_{\text{winner}} - v_{\text{2nd}} - 1,\; 0\bigr) $$

9. Listai mandatumelosztas (d'Hondt)

Kuszobertekek a nyers listai szavazatokra (toredek hozzaadasa elott):

  • Egy part: 5%
  • Ket part kozos lista: 10% (pl. Fidesz-KDNP)
  • Harom vagy tobb part: 15%

A kuszob feletti partok effektiv szavazata: $E_k = L_k + F_k$ (listai + toredek). A d'Hondt modszer a 93 listai mandatumot osztja el:

$$ \text{kvociens}_{k,s} = \frac{E_k}{s + 1}, \quad s = 0, 1, 2, \ldots $$

A 93 legnagyobb kvociens kap mandatumot.

10. Vegeredmeny

$$ \text{ossz. mandatum}_k = \text{OEVK}_k + \text{listai}_k $$

A $N$ szimulaciobol szamitjuk: mediant, 90%-os konfidencia-intervallumot ($P_5$–$P_{95}$), gyozelmi valoszinuseget ($\geq 100$ mandatum), es ketharmados eselyt ($\geq 133$).

  • Kozvelemenykutatasok: Median, Publicus, Zavecz Research, IDEA Intezet, Republikon, Nezopont, Szazadveg stb.

Korlatozasok

  • A modell a 2022-es keruleti eredmenyekre epit baziskent. Az uj partok (pl. Tisza) becslese a regi ellenzeki koalicio szavazatainak felosztasan alapul.
  • Nem modelezi a nemzetisegi listakat (1 mandatum).
  • A kozvelemenykutatasok hazeffektusainak becslese szubjektiv.
  • A swing modell feltetelez egyenletes viselkedest egy region belul; az urbanizacios korrekcioval a varos/videk kulonbseg lekezelheto.

Methodology

This simulator uses a Monte Carlo method to forecast the 2026 Hungarian parliamentary elections. The model runs $N = 20{,}000$ simulated elections, perturbing poll inputs with correlated random variables each time to assess uncertainty — an approach similar to Nate Silver's FiveThirtyEight model, adapted for the Hungarian mixed electoral system.

1. The Hungarian Electoral System

The National Assembly (Orszaggyules) has 199 seats:

  • 106 single-member district (SMD) seats — first-past-the-post (FPTP), single round. The candidate with the most votes wins the district.
  • 93 national list seats — proportional allocation via the d'Hondt method, augmented by fragment votes from SMD results.

2. Poll Aggregation

Each poll $i$ receives a composite weight that is the product of three factors:

$$ w_i = w_i^{\text{time}} \cdot w_i^{\text{sample}} \cdot w_i^{\text{quality}} $$

Recency weight — exponential decay with half-life $\tau$ (default: 14 days):

$$ w_i^{\text{time}} = \exp\!\Bigl(-\frac{\ln 2}{\tau} \cdot \Delta t_i\Bigr) $$

where $\Delta t_i$ is the number of days between the poll and the reference date.

Sample size weight:

$$ w_i^{\text{sample}} = \sqrt{\frac{n_i}{1000}} $$

Quality weight $w_i^{\text{quality}} \in [0, 1]$: a per-pollster reliability rating. House effect corrections (in percentage points) are applied to raw poll figures before averaging.

The weighted mean $\bar{\pi}_k$ for each party $k$ is normalized to sum to 1. The weighted standard deviation $\sigma_k^{\text{poll}}$ is computed for each party but floored at $\sigma_{\text{floor}}$ (default: 2%) to prevent overconfidence when few polls are available.

3. Correlated National Vote Share Draws

We transform the mean vote shares into logit space to ensure draws remain in $(0,1)$:

$$ \mu_k = \operatorname{logit}(\bar{\pi}_k) = \ln\!\frac{\bar{\pi}_k}{1 - \bar{\pi}_k} $$

The total logit-space standard deviation combines poll disagreement and systematic error:

$$ \sigma_k = \sigma_k^{\text{poll}} + \sigma_{\text{national}} $$

We construct a covariance matrix $\Sigma = \operatorname{diag}(\boldsymbol{\sigma}) \cdot R \cdot \operatorname{diag}(\boldsymbol{\sigma})$ where $R$ encodes the correlation structure:

  • Fidesz ↔ main opposition (Tisza): $\rho = -0.70$ (strong negative — when one gains, the other loses)
  • Small parties ↔ small parties: $\rho = +0.30$ (mild positive — anti-incumbent sentiment lifts all)
  • Fidesz ↔ small parties: $\rho = -0.20$

For each simulation $i$, we draw from a multivariate normal and add a correlated systematic polling error $\varepsilon$ (polls may all be wrong in the same direction):

$$ \mathbf{z}^{(i)} \sim \mathcal{N}(\boldsymbol{\mu}, \Sigma), \quad \varepsilon^{(i)} \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma_{\text{national}}) $$ $$ z_{\text{Fidesz}}^{(i)} \mathrel{+}= \varepsilon^{(i)}, \quad z_{\text{Tisza}}^{(i)} \mathrel{-}= \varepsilon^{(i)} $$

Shares are recovered via the sigmoid function and normalized:

$$ \pi_k^{(i)} = \frac{\operatorname{sigmoid}(z_k^{(i)})}{\sum_{j} \operatorname{sigmoid}(z_j^{(i)})} $$

4. Swing Model: National to District Translation

District-level vote shares are built from a 2022 baseline plus national swing, regional noise, and district-level noise:

$$ s_{d,k}^{(i)} = \underbrace{b_{d,k}}_{\text{2022 baseline}} + \underbrace{(\pi_k^{(i)} - \bar{b}_k)}_{\text{national swing}} + \underbrace{\eta_{r(d),k}^{(i)}}_{\text{regional noise}} + \underbrace{\epsilon_{d,k}^{(i)}}_{\text{district noise}} $$

where $b_{d,k}$ is the 2022 vote share of party $k$ in district $d$, $\bar{b}_k$ is the national 2022 baseline, and:

$$ \eta_{r,k} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\, \sigma_{\text{regional}}), \quad \epsilon_{d,k} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\, \sigma_{\text{district}}) $$

The 106 districts are grouped into 8 regions (Budapest, Pest, Northwestern Transdanubia, Central Transdanubia, Southern Transdanubia, Northern Hungary, Northern Great Plain, Southern Great Plain). Districts in the same region share the same regional noise draw $\eta_r$.

5. Urban/Rural Differential Swing

Each district $d$ is assigned an urbanization score $u_d \in [0, 1]$ (Budapest: 0.90–1.0, county capitals: 0.6–0.8, rural: 0.1–0.3). The centered urbanization $\tilde{u}_d = u_d - \bar{u}$ ensures the effect is zero-sum nationally:

$$ s_{d,k}^{(i)} \mathrel{+}= \gamma_k \cdot \tilde{u}_d $$

where $\gamma_k$ is the per-party urban swing parameter. For example, $\gamma_{\text{Fidesz}} = -0.05$ means Fidesz loses 5 percentage points in the most urban districts relative to the most rural ones, with no change in the national average.

Budapest extra swing: An additional parameter $\beta_{\text{BP}}$ applies exclusively to the 18 Budapest districts, modeling the capital's distinct political dynamics:

$$ s_{d,\text{Tisza}}^{(i)} \mathrel{+}= \beta_{\text{BP}} \cdot \mathbb{1}_{d \in \text{BP}}, \quad s_{d,\text{Fidesz}}^{(i)} \mathrel{-}= \beta_{\text{BP}} \cdot \mathbb{1}_{d \in \text{BP}} $$

After all adjustments, district shares are clipped to $[0.005,\, 0.95]$ and re-normalized to sum to 1.

6. Turnout Differentials

The 2022 district vote totals $V_d$ can be modified by urban/rural turnout shifts:

$$ V_d' = V_d \cdot \bigl(1 + \delta_{\text{urban}} \cdot u_d + \delta_{\text{rural}} \cdot (1 - u_d)\bigr) $$

where $\delta_{\text{urban}}$ and $\delta_{\text{rural}}$ are user-configurable turnout shift parameters. This models scenarios like "higher urban mobilization benefits opposition parties."

7. Ticket Splitting (List vs SMD Divergence)

Hungarian voters cast two ballots: one for a district (SMD) candidate and one for a national party list. These can diverge — e.g., some Mi Hazánk SMD voters might vote for an "other" candidate in the district but cast their list vote for Mi Hazánk. The model allows specifying, for each party, a target party and the fraction of SMD voters who split their list vote.

List vote shares are derived from the SMD baseline, adjusted by vote transfers:

$$ \text{list}_k^{(i)} = \pi_k^{(i)} - f_k \cdot \pi_k^{(i)} + \sum_{j:\,\text{target}(j)=k} f_j \cdot \pi_j^{(i)} $$

where $f_k \in [0, 0.30]$ is the fraction of party $k$'s SMD voters who cast their list vote for the designated target party instead. For example, if $f_{\text{Mi Hazank}} = 0.10$ with target $=$ other, then 10% of Mi Hazánk's SMD supporters vote "other" on the list. Default: 0 (no splitting).

8. SMD Simulation & Fragment Votes

In each district, the party with the most votes wins the seat. Fragment votes (toredekszavazatok), a unique feature of the Hungarian system, are computed as:

  • Losing candidates: all their votes become fragment votes for their party's national list.
  • Winning candidate:
$$ F_{\text{winner}} = \max\bigl(v_{\text{winner}} - v_{\text{2nd}} - 1,\; 0\bigr) $$

This mechanism reduces the "wasted vote" problem and compensates parties that lose many districts by narrow margins.

9. List Seat Allocation (d'Hondt)

Threshold eligibility is determined from raw list votes (before adding fragment votes):

  • Single party: 5%
  • Two-party joint list: 10% (e.g., Fidesz-KDNP)
  • Three-or-more party joint list: 15%

For eligible parties, the effective vote count is $E_k = L_k + F_k$ (list votes + fragment votes). The d'Hondt method allocates 93 seats by iteratively awarding seats to the party with the highest quotient:

$$ q_{k,s} = \frac{E_k}{s + 1}, \quad s = 0, 1, 2, \ldots $$

The 93 largest quotients across all eligible parties receive one seat each.

10. Final Results

$$ \text{total seats}_k = \text{SMD seats}_k + \text{list seats}_k $$

From the $N$ simulations we report: the median seat count, the 90% confidence interval ($P_5$–$P_{95}$), the win probability ($\Pr[\text{seats} \geq 100]$), and the supermajority probability ($\Pr[\text{seats} \geq 133]$).

Limitations

  • The model uses 2022 district results as baseline. New parties (e.g., Tisza) are estimated by splitting the 2022 united opposition vote using estimated split ratios.
  • Does not model nationality list seats (1 seat).
  • Pollster house effect estimates are subjective and based on historical comparisons.
  • The swing model assumes a uniform regional effect within each of the 8 regions; the urbanization correction addresses within-region urban/rural differences.
  • Candidate-level effects (personal vote, local incumbency advantage) are not modeled explicitly — they are captured as noise in $\sigma_{\text{district}}$.
